Who we are

We are a Virtual Airline founded to bring the rich heritage of Pakistan International Airlines to Infinite Flight. Our aim is to provide a high-quality platform for like-minded pilots to fly together. We offer a realistic career progression, extensive routes, and a friendly community.

Our Hubs

Pakistan International Airlines operates a network of hubs across Pakistan, with key airports serving as connections to domestic and international destinations.

Islamabad International Airport

ICAO: OPIS | Class: Bravo | Elevation: 1,761'

ISB serves as a major hub, offering strategic location for domestic and international connections.

Runways: 10L/28R (11,991') & 10R/28L

Jinnah International Airport

ICAO: OPKC | Class: Bravo | Elevation: 100'

The southern hub based in Sindh Province. KHI enables PVA to operate a comprehensive network of flights.

Runways: 07L/25R (10,491') & 07R/25L

Allama Iqbal International Airport

ICAO: OPLA | Class: Bravo | Elevation: 712'

Situated in Punjab province, offering great traffic by serving the populous region.

Runways: 18L/36R (11,013') & 18R/36L

The PVA Fleet

Legendary wings of PIA's heritage and modern giants of the global skies.

Airbus A320-200

M 0.78 | 3,300nm | 180 Pax

PIA Entry: 2014 (Narrow-body Modernisation)

Range Capability:

Heritage: Joined in 2014 to modernize the short-haul fleet. It's the backbone for domestic routes like Gilgit and Skardu.

Boeing 777-200ER

M 0.84 | 7,065nm | 313 Pax

PIA Entry: 2004 (First 777 Delivery)

Range Capability:

Heritage: Delivered in 2004, these jets revolutionized long-haul comfort on prime routes to London and Manchester.

Boeing 777-200LR

M 0.84 | 8,555nm | 317 Pax

PIA Entry: 2006 (Worldliner Launch Customer)

Range Capability:

Pride of Pakistan: PIA was the world's FIRST airline to fly the 777-200LR, enabling non-stop flights from Karachi to Toronto.

Boeing 777-300ER

M 0.84 | 7,370nm | 396 Pax

PIA Entry: 2006 (The Flagship Fleet)

Range Capability:

Heritage: The flagship of the fleet, known for its massive capacity. It's the primary choice for heavy Hajj operations.

Boeing 747-200 (Classic)

M 0.84 | 6,850nm | 452 Pax

PIA Entry: 1976 (The Golden Era)

Range Capability:

Heritage: A true legend from the '70s. PIA was one of the few modern airlines to fly the Queen of the Skies at that time.

McDonnell DC-10

M 0.82 | 5,200nm | 380 Pax

PIA Entry: 1974 (First Wide-body Jet)

Range Capability:

Heritage: The first wide-body jet in PIA's history, opening the doors to prestigious routes like Paris, Amsterdam, and New York.
